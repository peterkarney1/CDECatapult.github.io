Putting it all together

The end result of all the data munging, analysis, and data visualisation needs to be a compelling piece of work. Just doing a dashboard without supporting analysis and clear recommendations and calls for action won't bring about any results. Data doesn't speak for itself. You have to speak for it.

1

Step One - Figure out your story

Figure out what you want to say and what your most compelling top line numbers are and put them at the top.

Most people who look at things like this will have a quick glance at what they can see in the screen and move on. You have to capture them right away with something that will make them stop and want to read the rest.

2

Step Two - Everyone Loves a map

Maps are great if your data has a geographical component and are something you should always consider as people find them very compelling.

Time and again when I present users with a map the first thing they do is zoom directly to their own area. From there they usually check where they grew up or where other members of their family live. This is a great way to engage users and get them to dive into your data.

3

Step Three - Give them the details

After you have their attention you need to allow them to drill into the details so that they can better understand the underlying relationships in the data.

With our diabetes map there is a fair bit of variation, but without being able to drill into the details of the area you wouldn't know if the regional variation was because all of the surgeries in an area were the same or because of an enormous outlier in each CCG. Giving a detailed view of the underlying data helps with this.

4

Step Four - Show your work

Repeatability, traceability and provenance are essential to what we do here as data analysts and communicators. We need to be able to show where we get our data from and how we've processed it.

For public sites like this we can show what we've done in a github repository. For internal work you might have other ways of publishing what you've done.

People will make decisions that affect the world around you. Your work should be able to stand up to scrutiny.
